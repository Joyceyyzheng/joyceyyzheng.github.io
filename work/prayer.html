Prayer

Interactive Multimedia Installation, 2021
1m x 0.8m x 1.2m
TOOLkit
HTML, CSS, JavaScript
Illustrator, Arduino Kit, Raspberry Pi
Prayer is a multimedia installation that aims to reconstruct and embody the content and composition of superstitious activities in social media in the internet age. The installation consists of two main parts, an online webpage and an offline Arduino-powered mechanism and physical setup. Through the user's prayers on the webpage, the raspberry pi will conduct the signal, thus the fish tank (temporarily) placed in NYU Shanghai Room 826 will receive a gold coin.

Demo

Conceptual Development


‍



On Chinese social media, people like to retweet posts with pictures of koi, temple incense and blessings such as "whoever retweets this tweet will hear the best news of the month". In my opinion, this "internet blessing" is a revival of traditional blessings in the information age, assuming the role of a carrier of contemporary people's superstitious activities.

Prayer was thus born, aiming to make it possible for people all over the world to pray for something on the Internet. The physical part will be relatively traditional - when praying, especially with the "koi" imagery, people used to throw coins into the pond, which carried their good wishes.

*Pictures from Weibo

Technical Progress

I developed my project from two perspectives: the digital side and the analog side.

‍
The digital side composes a front-end website for users to view and the signal would be passed to the server with the user gesture. After receiving the signal, the arduino offline starts to work.



‍


For the physical part, I mainly focus on how to create a smooth mechanism where the golden can be dropped whenever triggered by the signal.
‍


‍

Future Improvement

The main improvement will focus on creating more interactions between the physical and the website part - as now the remote users can only see the coins number decreasing on the acrylic slipway but nothing else, it would be better if:
1. Add another camera capturing the process of coins falling down in the bowl, and show it to the user;
2. Add a projector projecting the [picture captured from user's camera] onto the water surface of the bowl, which also adds feedback to the user;
3. Instead of only showing the praying words on the screen, use a thermal printer that prints out the praying words, which will be destroyed by a shredder later;
4. Smoothen the physical mechanism.
