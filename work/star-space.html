Star space is a set of "empathy" themed toys for children and teens ages 5-14. Empathy refers firstly to sharing feelings of warmth, support and independence, and secondly to sharing knowledge of reactions to sexual abuse. The overall meaning is to help people lead better lives and gain mental strength and relief from plush toys. There is also an educational purpose when accessories are added. In general, players can play with the star as a sleep toy, put it in its room when needed, or DIY clothes/accessories for the star.

Introduction


‍

The star is a soft, bright yellow, human-shaped star with a red heart. The star's soft red heart can be opened and contains a 3D printed hard heart. The star also has a small, delicate room with a pillow, quilt, a book, a telephone, and a wooden knife. There are also positive adjectives engraved inside the room to encourage players. So far, the star has two sets of clothes, one is a loose and comfortable T-shirt, and the other is a sexy camisole and skirt. Players can choose to make their own bedding/clothes/scarves etc. for the stars.

“He has taken on the ‘essence’ of the kangaroo, says the savage; he is playing the kangaroo, say we. The savage, however, knows nothing of the conceptual distinctions between ‘being’ and ‘playing’; he knows nothing of ‘identity’ \ ‘image’ or ‘symbol’.”
Homo Ludens Chapter.1: nature and significance of play as a cultural phenomenon

When designing Star, the designer does not want the audience to feel like they are playing with it either, but more placing themselves in Star’s place, that’s also why Star has a room with a pillow and quilt, like a normal human being. What’s more, the toys has more social impact and reflection than we have thought:

“It opens up the possibility that children can explore and develop their diverse interests and skills, unconstrained by the dictates of gender stereotypes. And ultimately, isn’t that what we want for them?”
Toys Are More Divided by Gender Now Than They Were 50 Years Ago

Therefore, choosing Star as the representation not only helps to eliminate the discrimination society is putting on genders, but also broaden children’s horizons on their future development, keeping them away from being limited by their sex.

Workflow


I first started sketching to see what elements I wanted to include in the set, possible three views of the toy, and what it would eventually look like. After that, I digitized it into illustrator and dimensioned each part in great detail, as I'm handing it off to Sweet Home Shanghai to let them do the rest. For the "space" part, I also used illustrator and have it laser cut.

Toys will often need a package to promote themselves because they can't show everything to customers at once. Since the target customers of Star Space are children aged 5-14, I used stars made up of strokes as the background and adjusted the colors to make the whole picture look brighter and more vivid.
